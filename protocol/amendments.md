# Protocol Amendments

Record of changes to the original protocol, as required by PRISMA 2020.

| Date | Section Changed | Description of Change | Justification |
|------|-----------------|----------------------|---------------|
| 2026-03-23 | §7 Extraction | Added convention for fields without data: `NR` (not reported) when the paper does not mention the datum; `NA` (not applicable) when the field does not apply to the system. Affects mainly cost/efficiency fields. | The extraction pilot study revealed that cost fields are empty in 2/3 of papers, requiring a distinction between absence of data and inapplicability. |
| 2026-03-23 | §7 Extraction | Added clarifying note to the field `ameacas_validade`: infer from the reported limitations when the paper has no formal threats-to-validity section. | ML/AI papers rarely have a formal "threats to validity" section as in empirical SE. The pilot showed the need for a guide for inference. |
| 2026-03-23 | §6 Screening | Added note to the CI1 application guide: "When the abstract mentions 'long-term memory' or 'experience' without specifying cross-session persistence, classify as 'maybe' and verify in the full text." | The screening pilot identified 3/6 borderline papers where CI1 could not be determined from the abstract. |
| 2026-03-25 | §5 Search Strategy | Refinement of the search strings after systematic audit. **Concept 1:** added "code assistant", "agentic coding", "SWE agent". **Concept 2:** added "persistent memory", "persistent context", "cross-task". **arXiv:** corrected asymmetry with Scopus (4 C1 terms and 2 C2 terms absent without justification), added title-field search (`ti:`) for 4 key C1 terms. | Audit against 8 comparable SLRs (He et al. TOSEM 2024, Hou et al. TOSEM 2024, Jin et al. 2024, among others), 16 known systems, and 10 alternative term formulations. Seed-paper coverage improved from 4/7 to 6/7. arXiv volume: 876→973 (+11.1%). Terms evaluated and not added, with justification documented in `search/search-strategy.md`. |
| 2026-03-28 | §6 Screening | **Manual addition of seed paper not captured by the search.** CodeMEM (Wang et al. 2026, arXiv:2601.02868, S2 ID: 400f76b5dd2fb6e47b1f4db5c0b889a0b5135487) was identified as a seed paper during protocol formulation but was not captured by the search strings in Scopus or arXiv. The paper uses terms not covered by the strings ("AST-guided", "iterative code generation") and is categorized only under cs.CL in arXiv. Manually added to the screening pool. | The 6/7 seed-paper coverage was reported in the 2026-03-25 audit. CodeMEM was the 7th missing seed. The manual addition is PRISMA-compliant when documented as "records identified from other methods" and registered as an amendment. |
| 2026-03-26 | §3 Eligibility, §6 Screening | **Criteria stress test (v2.0).** Comprehensive review of the CI/CE criteria before screening 1,110 papers. Changes: **(1) CI1** — added detailed guide with explicit distinctions: explicit vs. parametric persistence (fine-tuning excluded), runtime vs. training time, agent vs. human (cursor rules excluded), positive/ambiguous/negative signals. **(2) CI2** — added minimum evaluation floor (≥2 tasks with metrics). **(3) CI5** — added note on papers <7 pages as "maybe". **(4) CE1** — expanded with explicit list of intra-session systems (context compression, prompt stuffing, file selection). **(5) CE2** — transformed into a conditional rule: generic papers included IF the evaluation includes SE benchmarks (inspired by Liu et al. TOSEM 2024 IC2). **(6) CE5** — added operationalization (title similarity >80%, preprint vs. venue rule). **(7)** Added 8 new borderline cases (#10–#17), totaling 17. **(8)** Created a decision flowchart with 8 sequential questions, signals per node, and three outcomes (include/exclude/maybe). **(9)** Defined quality gates and target metrics for the screening. | Stress test executed in 3 steps: (a) automated analysis of the 1,193-paper pool — 542 generic LLM-agent papers identified as the main grey zone (83% mention "agent", 30.2% do not fit any category); (b) benchmarking against 5 comparable SLRs (Hou et al. TOSEM 2024, Zhang et al. SCIS 2024, Jin et al. 2024, Wang et al. ASE 2024, Liu et al. TOSEM 2024) — identified: CE2 conditional rule, CI2 evaluation floor, CI5 minimum page count; (c) 18 adversarial cases across 7 boundaries — 5 critical gaps identified: lack of parametric/explicit distinction in CI1, lack of runtime/training distinction, agent/human ambiguity, absence of conditional rule in CE2, absence of floor in CI2. Full report: `screening/criteria-stress-test-report.md`. |
| 2026-04-24 | §3 Eligibility (IC1/EC1 boundary handling) | **Executable-self-evolution boundary rule.** Added a named exception to EC1 for systems that externalise procedural state into executable artefacts during task execution. Systems limited to intra-session textual retrieval remain excluded under EC1. A system may be retained as a boundary architecture study only when the artefact is created, invoked, and reused by the agent during task execution. Such systems are not treated as evidence of cross-session persistence unless the artefact persists across tasks. This amendment formalises the rationale for retaining Live-SWE-Agent (Xia et al., 2025) as a task-local executable procedural-memory boundary case. Corpus membership and PRISMA counts are unchanged. | External review (2026-04-24) observed that the original screening justification for Live-SWE-Agent ("evolves scaffold across tasks") did not match the paper digest, which is explicit that tools do not persist across tasks and each issue starts from the bare scaffold. The screening decision to include Live-SWE-Agent was retained, but the interpretation of IC1/EC1 was made explicit so the manuscript does not silently classify an intra-task system as cross-session evidence. The rule affects interpretation, not inclusion count. |
| 2026-04-24 | §8 Synthesis (SQ2 benchmark adoption count) | **SWE-bench Verified adoption count correction: 14 → 13.** The SQ2 text, Table 8, and the SQ2-A certainty rationale previously stated that 14 of the 24 benchmark-evaluated studies adopt SWE-bench Verified (58%). An audit against the extraction TSV (`extraction/extracted-data.tsv`, field `benchmarks_utilizados`) and per-paper digests shows that the correct count is 13 (54%). Hosain (Xolver, 2025) was incorrectly included: the paper evaluates on GSM8K, AIME 2024, AIME 2025, Math-500, and LiveCodeBench v5, and does not use SWE-bench Verified. The 13 studies that do are Chen, Deng, Guo, Li, Lindenbauer (subset), Shen (Structurally), Su, Wang (RepoMem), Wang (MemGovern), Wong, Wu, Xia, and Zhang (Darwin-Gödel). Zhou (ToM-SWE) is not counted because its benchmarks (Ambiguous SWE-bench and Stateful SWE) are tracked as separate rows in Table 8. Corpus membership, PRISMA flow counts, and eligibility criteria are unchanged; the synthesis matrix archived in the Zenodo v1.0 replication package retains the original count for provenance, and readers should consult this amendment log for the corrected figure. | Internal audit triggered by the external benchmark-inventory review (2026-04-24): expanding Table 8 into a full benchmark inventory made the misclassification of Hosain/Xolver visible. The correction is editorial (it affects derived counting, not corpus membership or extraction data), so no new Zenodo release was required for the TSV; the manuscript and the internal `extraction/digests/INDEX.md` router were updated, and this amendment documents the drift. |
